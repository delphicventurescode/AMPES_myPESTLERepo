## ðŸ§  The Science of "Flow"
Drawing from psychologist **Mihaly Csikszentmihalyi**, Swami Sarvapriyananda explains that our conscious capacity to process information is limited to approximately **120 bits per second**.

* **The Cause of Distraction:** Listening to one person takes roughly 60 bits. If you only commit 40 bits to a task, the remaining 80 bits of "surplus capacity" will naturally seek out distractions (phone, stray thoughts, etc.).
* **The Flow State:** Focus occurs when you pour nearly all of your 120-bit capacity into a single object, leaving no room for the mind to wander.

### 1. Active Reading (SQ3R Method)
Don't just "look" at the pages. Engage your curiosity using **SQ3R**:
* **Survey:** Quickly scan headings and diagrams (1 minute).
* **Question:** Ask "What do I want to learn here?" to engage your brain's search engine.
* **Read:** Read actively to find those answers.
* **Recite:** Stop and summarize what you read in your own words.
* **Review:** Go over the material to strengthen memory pathways.

### 2. The "Kaizen" Life
Focus on **incremental improvement**. Improve your "Head, Heart, and Hand" (thinking, feeling, and doing) by just a small percentage every single day.

### 3. Protect Your "Blade"
Think of your mind like a **razor**. If you use it to sharpen pencils all day (meaningless scrolling or constant distractions), it will be too blunt to "shave" (perform deep work) when you actually need it.
